Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between H.264, H.265, and ProRes?

H.264: Standard codec used by most cameras. Good balance of quality and file size.

H.265: Newer, more efficient. Smaller files at same quality, but requires compatible playback.

ProRes: Professional codec. Larger files, but optimized for editing workflows. Requires V90 cards.

What Speed Class do I need for 4K video?

V30 minimum for 4K 30fps or 1080p 60fps. Write speed of 30MB/s ensures smooth recording without dropped frames.

V60 recommended for 4K 60fps or higher frame rates. Future-proofs your card for newer cameras and demanding scenarios.

How do I know my camera's bitrate?

Method 1: Check your camera manual. Search for "bitrate" or "recording specs."

Method 2: Google "[Camera Model] bitrate." Reviews and tech sites often list this.

Method 3: Test record a short video, check file size, and our calculator has common presets.

Can I use the same card for multiple devices?

Yes, as long as the card meets the speed class and capacity needs of all your devices. We recommend dedicated cards for high-bitrate cameras and drones to avoid compatibility issues.

What if I need more storage during a shoot?

Bring backup cards. Always have a second card with the same specifications on-hand for long shoots or important events. Swapping cards takes seconds and ensures you never miss a shot.

How long do SD cards last?

Modern SD cards are rated for 10‚Äì20 years of normal use. For video storage, this is essentially forever. The real risk is accidental deletion or card failure ‚Äî always back up your footage immediately after a shoot.
