Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between H.264, H.265, and ProRes?

H.264: Standard codec used by most cameras. Good balance of quality and file size.

H.265: Newer, more efficient. Smaller files at same quality, but requires compatible playback.

ProRes: Professional codec. Larger files, but optimized for editing workflows. Requires V90 cards.

Why do I need overhead buffer?

SD cards reserve space for metadata, file system overhead, and write inefficiency. A 10–15% buffer (default) accounts for this. If you adjust to 0%, the card will work but may fail under stress.

What's the difference between Speed Class (V30, V60, V90)?

V30: Minimum 30MB/s write speed. Good for 4K 30fps and most video.

V60: Minimum 60MB/s write speed. Required for high-bitrate 4K 60fps video.

V90: Minimum 90MB/s write speed. Professional use, 8K video, or intensive workflows.

Higher class = more expensive, but smoother recording with less risk of dropped frames.

How do I check my camera's bitrate?

1. Check your camera's manual or specs page

2. Record a short video and check file properties

3. Search "[Camera Model] 4K bitrate" on YouTube — reviewers often list this

Not sure? Use the defaults in the calculator — they're appropriate for most cameras.

Can I use slower cards (Class 10) for 4K video?

Technically possible, but risky. Class 10 cards have no guaranteed write speed, so you may experience frame drops or failed recordings during intensive shooting. Always use the recommended Speed Class.

What if my calculation shows I need more storage than my card has?

You have two options:

1. Bring backup cards — Swap cards when full. Practical for trips.

2. Upgrade to larger capacity — Check the recommendations below for cards in your size range.
