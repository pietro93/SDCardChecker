Complete Guide: How to Spot a Fake SD Card

Counterfeit SD cards are programmed to lie about their capacity. A fake 256GB card might only have 16GB. Once you exceed the real limit, new files overwrite old ones. Data loss is permanent.

1. Source & Price Are Everything

Brands do not sell flagship cards at 80% off. If you bought from a marketplace seller at a suspiciously low price, the risk is extremely high.

2. Packaging Quality Matters

Official manufacturers spend serious money on packaging. Counterfeits cut corners. Look for blurry logos, spelling errors, missing holograms, or flimsy plastic.

3. Check the Card's Printing

Genuine cards have laser-etched text that is perfectly centered and sharp. Fakes have misaligned, fuzzy, or painted-on text. The plastic edges should be smooth, not rough.

4. The Definitive Test: Software Verification

This is the only 100% way to confirm. These tools write data to every sector and read it back to reveal the card's true capacity.

Windows: H2testw

Free German utility. Download, select your card's drive letter, run the test. It reports true capacity and any errors.

macOS: F3 (Fight Flash Fraud)

Free command-line tool. Same function as H2testw. Reliable Mac alternative.

⚠️ Important: Full tests take hours on large cards. It's destructive, so back up existing data first.

FAQ

Can Amazon sell fake cards?

"Sold by Amazon" = very low risk. "Sold by 3rd party" = higher risk. Always check the seller. Third-party sellers on the Marketplace sell more counterfeits.

What software should I use to test?

H2testw (Windows) and F3 (macOS) are the industry standards. Both are free and trusted. They perform a full read/write test to verify true capacity.

My fake card has photos on it. Can I recover them?

If you've written past the card's true capacity, newer files have overwritten older ones. That data is permanently gone. Try PhotoRec or Recuva to scan, but don't expect full recovery. Stop using the card immediately.
