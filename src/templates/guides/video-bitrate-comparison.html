H.264 vs H.265 vs ProRes: Understand Codec Choices

Learn how different video codecs affect bitrate, file size, and storage requirements. Understand which codec is best for your camera, use case, and workflow.

Understanding Video Codecs

A video codec is an algorithm that compresses video data to reduce file size while maintaining quality. The codec you choose affects how much storage you need, what bitrate your camera outputs, and whether professionals can use your files in their workflows.

This guide compares the three most common codecs: H.264 (industry standard), H.265 (modern efficient), and ProRes (professional quality).

Quick Codec Comparison

Codec 4K 30fps Bitrate File Size (per minute) Best For
H.264 100-150 Mbps 750 MB - 1.1 GB Consumer cameras, streaming
H.265 50-75 Mbps 375 MB - 560 MB Efficient storage, newer devices
ProRes 200-500 Mbps 1.5 GB - 3.75 GB Professional editing, cinema

H H.264 (MPEG-4 AVC)

Overview

H.264 is the most widely used video codec. It's been the industry standard for over 15 years and is supported by virtually all devices, cameras, and platforms.

Storage Impact

Requires more storage than H.265 due to less efficient compression. A 4K 30fps H.264 stream typically uses 100-150 Mbps.

Best For

  • Consumer cameras and smartphones
  • Streaming (YouTube, social media)
  • Maximum device compatibility
  • Established workflows

Speed Class Needed: V30 minimum for 4K 30fps H.264

H H.265 (HEVC)

Overview

H.265 (High Efficiency Video Coding) is the modern successor to H.264. It provides better compression, achieving the same quality at ~50% lower bitrate.

Storage Impact

Significantly reduces storage needs. A 4K 30fps H.265 stream uses only 50-75 Mbps, roughly half of H.264.

Best For

  • Newer cameras and phones
  • Storage-constrained workflows
  • Longer recording sessions
  • 4K and 8K content

Speed Class Needed: V30 for 4K 30fps H.265 (same as H.264)

P ProRes (Professional)

Overview

ProRes is Apple's professional codec designed for post-production editing. It uses higher bitrates than H.264/H.265 for maximum quality and editing flexibility.

Storage Impact

Requires significantly more storage. ProRes 4K 30fps uses 200-500 Mbps depending on variant (ProRes 422, ProRes HQ, etc.).

Best For

  • Professional cinema production
  • High-end editing workflows
  • Color grading and effects
  • Archival quality footage

Speed Class Needed: V60-V90 for ProRes recording (higher bitrates)
