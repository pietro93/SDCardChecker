How to Use an SD Card Reader with iPhone 15

1

Get Files App Ready

Open the Files app on your iPhone (pre-installed). This is where your SD card will appear when connected.

2

Connect Reader to iPhone

Plug the USB-C reader into your iPhone's USB-C port. You may see a popup asking for permission‚Äîtap "Trust" to allow access.

3

Insert SD Card

Insert your SD card into the reader. The card should appear in the Files app under "Locations" (might take a few seconds).

4

Copy Files

Navigate to your photos or videos in the Files app. Select files and copy them to iPhone's storage (Photos app or Documents folder).

5

Safe Eject

Before removing the reader, eject the card from Files app. Long-press the card name and select "Eject" to safely disconnect.

Pro Tip: iPhone works best with USB 3.0 readers (like SanDisk Extreme PRO). Regular USB 2.0 readers are slower but still work fine. Copy speeds: 10-30 MB/s on USB 2.0, 50-100+ MB/s on USB 3.0.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will any USB-C reader work with iPhone 15?

Yes, any standard USB-C reader works with iPhone 15. No special Apple drivers needed. However, USB 3.0 readers are faster than USB 2.0 readers on iPhone 15 Pro models.

Can I edit photos directly from the SD card on iPhone?

Not really. iOS requires you to copy photos to iPhone's storage first. You can view them in Files app, but most photo editing apps work best with files in Photos app or your Documents folder.

Is it better to use USB-C or USB-A readers on iPhone?

USB-C is better: iPhone 15 has USB-C, so native USB-C readers connect directly without adapters. USB-A readers require a USB-A to USB-C adapter (slower, more hassle).

Do I need a fast reader for iPhone?

Not essential. Even slow readers (USB 2.0) work with iPhone. However, if you regularly transfer large batches of photos or videos, a fast USB 3.0 reader on iPhone 15 Pro saves significant time.

Can I use the same reader for iPhone and computer?

Absolutely. Most USB-C readers work with both iPhone 15 and Mac/Windows computers. Dual-connector readers (like UGREEN) also work with older USB-A computers. One reader for everything is perfect.
