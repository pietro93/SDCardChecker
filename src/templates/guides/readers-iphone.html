Frequently Asked Questions

Why does iPhone limit USB-C speeds?

iPhone 15 standard models use a bottleneck controller that limits USB to ~60 MB/s. iPhone 15 Pro models use USB 3.0 for full-speed access. It's a hardware limitation, not a reader issue.

Can I charge my iPhone while using a reader?

Not simultaneously‚ÄîiPhone only has one USB-C port. However, you can plug into power after transferring files. Most transfers complete in minutes, so it's rarely an issue.

Will a flexible cable reader damage my phone?

No. Flexible cables actually reduce stress on the port by absorbing physical impacts. This is why Apple's official reader uses a cable, not a rigid connector.

Why don't I recommend the Anker PowerExpand here?

Anker PowerExpand is a good reader, but it's a dual USB-C/USB-A dongle with a rigid design. For iPhone specifically, the cable-style readers (Apple, SanDisk, Transcend) are safer and better optimized.
