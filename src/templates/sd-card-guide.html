Complete SD Card Guide

Everything you need to know about SD cards, from types and speeds to compatibility and best practices.

SD Card Types

SD (Secure Digital)

Capacity: Up to 2GB

The original SD card format with limited storage capacity. Largely obsolete and rarely used in modern devices.

SDHC (Secure Digital High Capacity)

Capacity: 4GB to 32GB

The most common SD card format for decades. Still widely supported but being replaced by SDXC in modern devices. Compatible with all SDHC-enabled devices.

SDXC (Secure Digital eXtended Capacity)

Capacity: 32GB to 2TB

The modern standard for high-capacity SD cards. Offers significantly larger storage and faster speeds. Recommended for most new devices. Note: Some older devices may not support SDXC.

microSD

Capacity: 128MB to 2TB | Size: 15mm × 11mm × 1mm

A miniaturized version of SD cards used in smartphones, tablets, action cameras, and drones. Often includes an adapter to use as a full-size SD card.

Speed Classes Explained

Speed classes guarantee minimum write speeds for recording video. Different standards measure different aspects of performance:

U Speed Class (UHS Speed Class)

Minimum sustained write speed

U1: 10 MB/s minimum
U3: 30 MB/s minimum

Recommended for most users. U3 is ideal for 4K video recording.

V Speed Class (Video Speed Class)

Minimum sustained write speed for video

V6: 6 MB/s
V30: 30 MB/s
V60: 60 MB/s

Specifically designed for video recording. V30 is ideal for 4K, V60 for professional work.

Speed Class (Original)

Minimum sustained write speed

Class 2: 2 MB/s
Class 4: 4 MB/s
Class 6: 6 MB/s
Class 10: 10 MB/s

Older standard, less commonly used today. Class 10 is the highest rating.

Bus Speed (UHS Bus Interface)

Maximum theoretical transfer speeds

UHS-I: Up to 104 MB/s
UHS-II: Up to 312 MB/s
UHS-III: Up to 624 MB/s

Higher bus speeds enable faster read/write operations. Requires compatible device.

How to Choose the Right SD Card

1

Check Your Device's Requirements

Verify the maximum capacity and speed your device supports. Some older devices may not support SDXC cards.

2

Determine Your Use Case

Different activities require different speeds: 4K video needs at least U3/V30, photography needs fast random writes, everyday storage needs basic speed.

3

Choose the Right Capacity

Get enough storage for your needs plus extra buffer. Consider that actual capacity is slightly less than advertised due to formatting.

4

Buy From Reputable Brands

Stick to well-known manufacturers like SanDisk, Kingston, Lexar, and Samsung to ensure authenticity and quality.

Best Practices for SD Cards

Protect Your Data

  • Always back up important files
  • Use reliable card readers
  • Avoid removing card while writing
  • Keep cards in protective cases

Maintenance Tips

  • Store in cool, dry environment
  • Avoid extreme temperatures
  • Keep away from magnetic fields
  • Use proper ejection procedures

Before Purchase

  • Verify device compatibility
  • Check speed ratings needed
  • Compare prices across brands
  • Read recent reviews

Avoid Common Mistakes

  • Don't buy counterfeit cards
  • Don't exceed capacity limits
  • Don't use unsupported formats
  • Don't ignore speed warnings
