Official Lenovo Legion Go SD Card Requirements

  • Format: microSD UHS-I
  • Minimum Speed: U3 / V30 (30 MB/s write)
  • App Performance: A2 (Required for OS/Apps)
  • Maximum Capacity: Up to 2TB
Why these requirements? A Windows gaming handheld featuring a large 8.8-inch display and detachable controllers. An A2 application performance rating on the microSD card significantly improves game load times and overall system responsiveness when running games from the card. The large screen and Windows OS make it suitable for AAA gaming, which demands substantial storage capacity.

Frequently Asked Questions

What SD Card Do I Need for Lenovo Legion Go?
The Lenovo Legion Go requires a microSD UHS-I card with U3 / V30 speed rating for reliable performance. We recommend 1TB capacity as the sweet spot balancing storage capacity with affordability. The device supports up to 2TB, though most users find 1TB sufficient for daily use. Always choose from trusted brands like SanDisk, Lexar, or Kingston to ensure consistent performance and avoid data loss.
Legion Go vs ROG Ally - which has better SD card performance?
The ASUS ROG Ally X features a UHS-II card reader, while the Lenovo Legion Go utilizes a UHS-I slot. This means the Ally X can achieve faster game load times with UHS-II cards. However, the Legion Go offers unique features like its larger screen and detachable controllers, appealing to different user preferences. Both devices benefit greatly from large-capacity (512GB+) A2-rated cards for modern games.
What microSD card capacity do I need for PC games on the Legion Go?
A 512GB card is a good minimum, but 1TB is highly recommended for an extensive library of modern PC games. Many AAA titles can easily exceed 100GB each. While the Legion Go's Windows OS allows flexible storage management, microSD card space can fill up quickly with a few large titles, making higher capacity a wise investment.
Is V30 required for Lenovo Legion Go?
Yes, V30 is recommended for Lenovo Legion Go. It guarantees a minimum sustained write speed of 30 MB/s, which is necessary for stable professional recording without dropped frames or errors.
What storage capacity should I get for Lenovo Legion Go?
We recommend 512GB or 1TB cards. A 512GB card is good for typical use, with 2TB as the maximum supported capacity. Larger sizes are useful if you shoot frequently and want to minimize card swaps.
Can I use older or slower cards with Lenovo Legion Go?
Not recommended. Cards slower than U3 / V30 may cause dropped frames, corrupted files, or recording failures. Always use U3 / V30 minimum for reliability.
Do I need a UHS card for Lenovo Legion Go?
UHS cards are recommended for best performance with Lenovo Legion Go. Non-UHS cards will work but may have slower transfer speeds. For this device, UHS-II is optimal.
Should I use more than one card with Lenovo Legion Go?
For professional use or extended shooting sessions, dual cards provide redundancy and backup. Using multiple cards ensures you won't lose footage if one card fails. This is especially important for valuable recordings.
Does the brand matter for Lenovo Legion Go?
Yes, trusted brands like SanDisk, Lexar, and Kingston are recommended. Quality brands have better reliability and warranty support. Avoid unknown brands, especially for demanding devices.
What happens if I use the wrong card with Lenovo Legion Go?
Using cards slower than U3 / V30 can cause: dropped frames during recording, corrupted files, or complete recording failure. Stick to U3 / V30 minimum to avoid data loss.
How long will an SD card last with Lenovo Legion Go?
Quality SD cards typically last 3-5 years with normal use. Replace your card if you experience read/write errors, corrupted files, or if it's been dropped or exposed to extreme conditions.
