Official Skydio 2+ SD Card Requirements

  • Format: microSD UHS-I / UHS-II
  • Minimum Speed: V30 / U3 (30 MB/s write)
  • Maximum Capacity: Up to 256GB (Official), some 400GB+ supported on newer firmware
Why these requirements? The Skydio 2+ relies heavily on the SD card not just for 4K/60fps video, but for data logging its complex autonomous flight pathing. Skydio is notorious for rejecting cards that do not meet strict U3/V30 speed tests. The file system must be exFAT.

Frequently Asked Questions

What SD Card Do I Need for Skydio 2+?
The Skydio 2+ requires a microSD UHS-I / UHS-II card with V30 / U3 speed rating for reliable performance. We recommend 256GB capacity as the sweet spot balancing storage capacity with affordability. The device supports up to 256GB (Official), some 400GB+ supported on newer firmware, though most users find 256GB sufficient for daily use. Always choose from trusted brands like SanDisk, Lexar, or Kingston to ensure consistent performance and avoid data loss.
My Skydio won't take off and complains about the SD card?
Skydio drones perform a speed test on the card before flight. If the card is too slow (Class 10 or U1) or fragmented, the drone will prevent takeoff to save your data. You must use a high-quality V30/U3 card.
Does Skydio support UHS-II?
Yes, Skydio 2+ supports UHS-II cards. While not strictly required for recording, UHS-II cards can offer faster offload speeds to your computer.
Is V30 required for Skydio 2+?
Yes, V30 is recommended for Skydio 2+. It guarantees a minimum sustained write speed of 30 MB/s, which is necessary for stable professional recording without dropped frames or errors.
What storage capacity should I get for Skydio 2+?
We recommend 128GB or 256GB cards. A 128GB card is good for typical use, with 256GB (Official), some 400GB+ supported on newer firmware as the maximum supported capacity. Larger sizes are useful if you shoot frequently and want to minimize card swaps.
Can I use older or slower cards with Skydio 2+?
Not recommended. Cards slower than V30 / U3 may cause dropped frames, corrupted files, or recording failures. Always use V30 / U3 minimum for reliability.
Do I need a UHS card for Skydio 2+?
UHS cards are recommended for best performance with Skydio 2+. Non-UHS cards will work but may have slower transfer speeds. For this device, UHS-II is optimal.
Should I use more than one card with Skydio 2+?
For professional use or extended shooting sessions, dual cards provide redundancy and backup. Using multiple cards ensures you won't lose footage if one card fails. This is especially important for valuable recordings.
Does the brand matter for Skydio 2+?
Yes, trusted brands like SanDisk, Lexar, and Kingston are recommended. Quality brands have better reliability and warranty support. Avoid unknown brands, especially for demanding devices.
What happens if I use the wrong card with Skydio 2+?
Using cards slower than V30 / U3 can cause: dropped frames during recording, corrupted files, or complete recording failure. Stick to V30 / U3 minimum to avoid data loss.
How long will an SD card last with Skydio 2+?
Quality SD cards typically last 3-5 years with normal use. Replace your card if you experience read/write errors, corrupted files, or if it's been dropped or exposed to extreme conditions.
