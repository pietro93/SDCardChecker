Frequently Asked Questions

Why is USB 4.0 faster than Thunderbolt 3?

Thunderbolt 3 and USB 4.0 both have 40Gbps maximum bandwidth. However, most "Thunderbolt 3 readers" are actually USB 3.2 Gen 2 devices (10Gbps) that plug into Thunderbolt ports via an adapter. The ProGrade USB 4.0 is a true USB 4.0 device, unlocking the full 40Gbps bandwidth for CFexpress cards.

Can I use a USB 4.0 reader on a Thunderbolt 3 Mac?

Yes, backward compatible. But you'll only get 10Gbps speeds (USB 3.2 fallback), not the full 40Gbps. For max CFexpress speeds, use MacBook Pro M3/M4 (Thunderbolt 4) or the older PG05.5 reader on Thunderbolt 3 Macs.

Is the Kingston Workflow Station worth $350?

Yes, if you shoot multi-camera events. A wedding with 2 photographers (4 cards): saving 36 minutes per shoot = 3‚Äì4 events per year justifies the cost. For single-camera shooters, it's overkill.

What makes the SanDisk PRO-READER's write-protect switch special?

It's physical hardware-level protection, not software. Even if someone clicks "Format" or "Delete," the card is locked at the device level. Essential for studios handing cards to assistants. No other reader has this feature.
